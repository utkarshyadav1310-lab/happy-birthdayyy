Patrakar Ka Birthday

Happy Birthday, Jaya ❤️

With love, always — Patrakar

For the Day You Were Born I am not a good man in the cinematic sense. I forget to reply sometimes. I sleep when I should stay awake for you. I choose silence when words are needed most. If love were a discipline, I would be the student always late, shirt untucked, heart in his hands, asking for one more chance. I don’t come with roses every morning. Some days I arrive empty, like a cup left too long on the table. I know this. I see it in the pauses between your messages, in the way your warmth sometimes has to travel miles before reaching me. And yet— God, and yet. When I think of you, the world softens at the edges. Even my failures learn to kneel. You turn ordinary hours into something holy, like Keats’ nightingale singing from a place too tender to touch, too real to doubt. I love you in ways that don’t always behave. I love you with a heart that stumbles, with hands that shake while holding truth. I love you not as a promise of perfection but as a vow of return— that even when I fall short, I will walk back to you, again and again. On your birthday, I don’t offer grand futures or flawless days. I offer this: a man still learning how to love better, a man who knows your presence is not a convenience but a miracle, not a habit but a choice I would make in every lifetime. If I have hurt you, know it was never from lack of love but from the clumsiness of carrying something so precious with untrained hands. I am trying. I am staying. I am yours, in all my broken verbs and unfinished lines. Today, the earth celebrates the fact that you arrived. And selfishly, quietly, so do I— because somehow, in this loud and careless world, you chose to stay with me. Happy Birthday. If I am still becoming, it is because loving you has taught me how.
